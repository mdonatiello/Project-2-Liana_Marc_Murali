Project Description

This project analyzed 2075 records on New York State transportation construction projects.

Dataset:

NYS Transportation Projects in Your Neighborhood (from the Open NY Dataset)
• CSV versions of the data were accessed from Kaggle.com.
• A JSON file for the same dataset was accessed for an API pull into MongoDB.

group

Questions Asked

• Does the data suggest a region might be underinvesting in transportation infrastructure?

• Is this region's infrastructure in need of increased investment?

settings

Significant Finding

New York City was last in terms of:
• total number of projects
• total amount of funding
• projects in future development.

New York City was tied for second to last with Mohawk Valley in terms of projects currently in development, ahead of only the Capital District.

Insight!

The majority of funding in each region came from state sources followed by federal funding at roughly the same proportion to state funding in each region. Local funding was significantly less than state and federal funding in all regions. Long Island and Hudson Valley were numbers 1 and 2 respectively in terms of total funding..



News

New York City's time at the bottom of most of the rankings may come to an end in the next few years as the Metropolitan Transportation Authority announced on September 16, 2019, a $51.5 billion proposed plan to upgrade subways, buses and the commuter rails Metro North and the Long Island Railroad. A large percentage of the funding will come from local funding, specifically congestion pricing and the new taxes that were passed along with it.
